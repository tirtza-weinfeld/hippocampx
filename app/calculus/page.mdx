# Calculus

## Resizable Table of Contents

## **1. Functions**

A function assigns each input a single output.

* **Single-variable:**
  $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$
  Example: *[green!]$f(x)=x^2+3x$*

* **Multivariable:**
  $F:\mathbb{R}^n\to\mathbb{R}$
  Example: *[green!]$F(x,y)=x^2+xy+y^3$*

---

## **2. Limits**

A limit describes the value a quantity approaches.

Example:

$$
\lim_{h\to 0}(x+h)=x.
$$

---

## **3. Derivative (1D)**

A function $f$ is differentiable at $a$ if the limit

$$
f'(a)=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}
$$

exists and is finite.

### **Tangent Line**

$$
T(x)=f(a)+f'(a)(x-a).
$$

### **Basic Derivative Rules**

$$
\frac{d}{dx}(x^n)=n x^{n-1},
\qquad
\frac{d}{dx}(e^x)=e^x,
\qquad
\frac{d}{dx}(\ln x)=\frac{1}{x}.
$$

---

## **4. Chain Rule (1D)**

If $h(x)=f(g(x))$, then

$$
h'(x)=f'(g(x)),g'(x).
$$

---

## **5. Multivariable Functions**

A function of several inputs is written:

$$
F(x_1,x_2,\dots,x_n).
$$

---

## **6. Partial Derivatives**

Definition:

$$
\frac{\partial F}{\partial x_i}(x_1,\dots,x_n)

==============================================

\lim_{h\to 0}
\frac{
F(x_1,\dots,x_i+h,\dots,x_n)
----------------------------

F(x_1,\dots,x_i,\dots,x_n)
}{h}.
$$

Example with $F(x,y)=x^2+xy+y^3$:

$$
\frac{\partial F}{\partial x}=2x+y,
\qquad
\frac{\partial F}{\partial y}=x+3y^2.
$$

---

## **7. Linear Approximation**

For small $h\in\mathbb{R}^n$:

$$
F(x+h)\approx F(x)+\nabla F(x)\cdot h.
$$

This is the multivariable analogue of a tangent line.

---

## **8. Gradient**

The gradient of $F:\mathbb{R}^n\to\mathbb{R}$ is:

$$
\nabla F(x_1,\dots,x_n)
=======================

\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{\partial F}{\partial x_1} \
\frac{\partial F}{\partial x_2} \
\vdots \
\frac{\partial F}{\partial x_n}
\end{bmatrix}.
$$

Key properties:

* Direction of maximum increase
* Magnitude = maximum rate of increase
* Contains all first-order change information

---

## **9. Directional Derivative**

For direction $v\in\mathbb{R}^n$:

$$
D_v F(x)=\nabla F(x)\cdot v.
$$

---

## **10. Critical Points**

A point $x^*$ is a **critical point** if

$$
\nabla F(x^*)=0.
$$

Possible types:

* local minimum
* local maximum
* saddle point

---

## **11. Steepest Descent**

To decrease $F$, move opposite the gradient:

$$
x_{\text{new}}
==============

## x_{\text{old}}

\eta,\nabla F(x_{\text{old}}),
$$

where $\eta>0$ is the step size.
